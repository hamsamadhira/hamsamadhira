The things that shape me

Music, Travel, and the Science of Curiosity

The things I care about most are often the things that make life feel alive: music, travel, learning, and understanding people more deeply. Music has always been one of the strongest influences in my life. I love the way a song can capture a feeling that words sometimes cannot. Music can be energizing, comforting, cinematic, nostalgic, or grounding depending on the moment. I’m especially drawn to live performances because they turn music into a shared experience. There is something powerful about being in a room where everyone is feeling the same rhythm, the same emotion, or the same anticipation at once. That kind of connection is one of the reasons music matters so much to me.

Travel is another major source of inspiration for me. I love how new places shift your perspective. Every city has its own personality — its streets, colors, architecture, food, sounds, and pace. Traveling teaches you to observe more carefully. It makes you notice small details and appreciate how different people create meaning, beauty, and routine in their lives. Some of my favorite moments are the simple ones: walking through a place I’ve never seen before, finding a view that makes me stop, or hearing a song that permanently becomes attached to a trip.

My academic interests in computer science and neuroscience connect naturally to all of this. Computer science teaches me how systems work, how ideas become tools, and how structure can create innovation. Neuroscience teaches me how humans think, remember, feel, and respond to the world around them. Together, these two fields feel incredibly meaningful to me because they combine problem-solving with human understanding. One explores machines and logic; the other explores the mind and behavior. Both are ultimately about patterns, connection, and discovery.

When I think about who I am, I think about someone who wants to build a life that is both intellectually exciting and deeply lived. I want to keep exploring new places, learning new ideas, and creating things that reflect both creativity and intention. That’s what this page represents: a small collection of the interests that inspire me most and continue to shape the way I see the world.

Things That Inspire Me

  • Traveling to new cities and countries
  • Neuroscience and the mysteries of the brain
  • Building projects in computer science
  • Photography and visually memorable moments
  • Creative websites with strong personality
