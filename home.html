Hi, I’m Hamsa. I’m a Computer Science and Neuroscience student who loves building things that feel both technically sharp and deeply human. I’m especially excited by projects where software does more than function — where it can adapt, reason thoughtfully, and support people in meaningful ways. That’s part of why I enjoy work in agentic AI systems, machine learning pipelines, and workflow automation.

I’m drawn to projects that sit at the intersection of AI, systems thinking, and real-world impact, especially in health-tech and workflow automation. I’m interested in how intelligent systems can reduce friction, improve decision-making, and create better human experiences, especially when paired with an understanding of behavior, memory, and the brain.

Outside of code, I’m someone who feels deeply connected to music, travel, performance, and visual atmosphere. I notice color, sound, movement, architecture, and the small details that make a moment memorable. This site reflects all of those different sides of me — the builder, the performer, the observer, the collector, and the person constantly curious about both people and technology.
