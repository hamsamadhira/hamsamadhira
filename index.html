Hi, I'm Hamsa.

I’m a Computer Science and Neuroscience student who loves building, exploring, and finding meaning in both data and everyday experiences.

Welcome to my little corner of the internet. I wanted this website to feel less like a formal assignment and more like a snapshot of who I am. I’m someone who is constantly moving between different worlds: technology and creativity, logic and emotion, structure and spontaneity. On one side, I’m deeply interested in computer science and the way technology can solve real problems, create new tools, and transform how people connect with information. On the other side, I’m equally drawn to neuroscience, because understanding the brain means understanding memory, behavior, emotion, and what makes us human.

Outside the classroom, some of the things I love most are music and travel. Music is a huge part of how I experience life. It shapes my mood, helps me focus, and makes moments feel bigger and more memorable. Whether it’s live performances, discovering new artists, or replaying songs that feel tied to a particular season of life, music always finds a way to stay with me. Travel inspires me in a similar way. I love seeing new cities, noticing architecture, collecting small moments, and experiencing how different places have their own energy and rhythm.

This website brings those parts of me together. It’s about who I am, what I care about, and the ideas that keep me curious. I wanted the design to feel warm, expressive, and colorful while still being clean and easy to navigate. On the next page, I share more about the interests that shape me most — from music and travel to the connection between computer science and neuroscience.
